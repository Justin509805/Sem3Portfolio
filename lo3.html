RESEARCH METHODS & AREAS

I defined a general guideline of which research methods and areas that we will be doing. Inside the research plan you can find which research areas and methods we will cover in each phase of the Double Diamond. We decided that we would be able to change this plan so we can be more flexible.

Gitwiki about research plan

Summary of the explanation

Discover Phase:

Research Areas:

  • User research: Who are the users? What are their needs?
  • Market research: Who are the competitors, what are the market trends?
  • Problem discovery: What’s the root cause of the problem?

Research Methods:

  • Interviews/surveys: To gain insights from users.
  • Personas: Based on interview/survey results.
  • Primary research: Conduct new research.
  • Secondary research: Conduct already done research.
  • User observation: Observe users in fast fashion shops.
  • Brainstorming: We did brain writing and lotus blossom.

Define Phase:

Research Areas:

  • Problem framing: Converge data into a clear problem statement.
  • User needs: Define and prioritize user needs.
  • Validate assumptions: Make sure our assumptions align with user needs.

Research Methods:

  • Journey mapping: Which steps do our users take to accomplish a goal?
  • SWOT analysis: Define strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
  • Co-creation: Sit down with the user and collaborate to guide the design process.

Develop Phase:

Research Areas:

  • Ideate and create a concept.
  • Prototyping: Create low to high fidelity prototypes.
  • Usability testing: Test the prototypes for feedback.
  • Iterate and improve: Make iterations based on feedback.

Research Methods:

  • Usability testing: Test prototypes.
  • Prototyping: Create prototypes for testing.
  • A/B testing: Test two different versions and compare results.

Deliver Phase:

Research Areas:

  • Track metrics of success and key performance indicators (KPIs): Measure project outcomes.
  • Refinement and validation: Fine-tune based on feedback and testing.

Research Methods:

  • Pitching to the client: Presenting the final solution to the client.
  • Usability testing: Continue testing for final improvements.
  • Code review: Review code for quality and optimization.
