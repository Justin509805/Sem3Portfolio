Link Between Learning Outcomes (LO) and What I Did:

Fitness apps are often made with multiple health features such as sleep, fitness, and nutrition.

  • Personalization: Light and Dark mode is a trend which has been around for a while.
  • Gamification: Badges, ranks, and progress bars are often used to keep users engaged.

Exploring emerging design trends:

You create engaging concepts and translate them into interactive validated media products by applying user-centered design principles, visual design techniques...

User-Centered Design Principles:

  • Keeping it consistent: I used the same color combinations throughout all the pages, including having the same main color green in both the light and dark modes.
  • Use plain language: I made sure to type all of the content myself and have it written in simple English. Think of the lines of motivation, for example.
  • Reduce user effort: I made it easy for the user to find where certain things are. For instance, it's very simple to add a new measure of your weight because of the large plus icon. It is also easy to find the light and dark mode switch.
  • Provide feedback: If something is clickable, an animation of some sort will be provided. Implementation of popups is also being considered.
  • Simplify navigation: Five navigation buttons allow users to move through all the pages. For example, users can't go to the calories page by clicking on the calories section on the dashboard.

Visual Design Techniques:

  • Color Theory: Green is often associated with success, progress, and positivity, which fits the purpose of the app.
  • Typography Hierarchy: Some important features have a bigger weight, such as the amount of weight lost or gained and the number of reps and weight completed.
  • White Space: I added white space around the edges of the screen so the focus is more on the app. I also implemented white space in places that often feel too cluttered.
  • Contrast & Emphasis: Previously, the blue had too much contrast, which shifted emphasis to the background instead of the content. With a muted green color, the white text inside has more contrast, shifting the emphasis to the content.
